SCENE 01 Discovering the Stones

EXT. OLD TASHKENT ALLEYWAY — DUSK. The old quarter of the city smells of spice markets and electric air. Under a crumbled section of pavement — four glowing stones, pulsing with slow cosmic rhythm. The team approaches.

AMIRBEK
Wait — everyone stop. Do you hear that? It's like… a voice. It's coming from the ground.
SHAHINA
A voice? Amirbek, you're hearing things. It's probably a broken gas pipe or some underground cable.
KRISTINA MY LINE
No, wait — she's wrong. I hear it too. Something is whispering. And those stones… they're glowing.
AMIRBEK
We pick them up. All four of us, at the same time. On three. One… two… three!
KRISTINA MY LINE
(grabs hers without hesitation) Already done. (the stone blazes in her hand) And it's warm — not just warm. It feels like it's alive.
AKBARHON
Bold plan, fearless leader! What could possibly go wrong?
KRISTINA MY LINE
Everything could go wrong. That's never stopped us before. (grins) Let's move — whatever this is, it wants to be found.
SCENE 02 Decoding the Star Messages

INT. SHAHINA'S BEDROOM — NIGHT. Code streams across every surface. The city sprawls through the window, unaware of what the four teens now know. Kristina paces, too restless to sit, constantly scanning the room like she's already planning an escape route.

SHAHINA
I've isolated the frequency. These are structured pulses — binary, but with a third state. Whoever sent this is not from Earth.
AMIRBEK
Then what are they saying? We need to know if we're in danger.
KRISTINA MY LINE
Look at the map — the signal is pointing directly at Tashkent. We're the target.
AMIRBEK
Target for what? Are they warning us, or marking us?
KRISTINA MY LINE
(studying the star map projection) I've traced the signal origin. It's coming from the Vega system — 25 light-years away. They sent this message 25 years ago, knowing we'd be ready now.
AKBARHON
They planned for us? That's either incredibly flattering or very terrifying.
KRISTINA MY LINE
Both. (grabs her jacket) We need to get to the highest point in Tashkent. If the convergence is atmospheric, that's where we make our stand. Chorsu rooftop — I know a way up.
SCENE 03 Cosmic Danger

EXT. CHORSU ROOFTOP — MIDNIGHT. The rift tears the sky open. Wind howls through the gap in reality. Kristina stands closest to the edge, staring up at the descending void without flinching. Her stone blazes violet-white.

KRISTINA MY LINE
It's here. It's actually here. The rift is 200 metres wide and growing. Amirbek — what do we do?!
AMIRBEK
We stand our ground. I am NOT letting that happen!
SHAHINA
The stones resonate at the same frequency as the entity's core. If we synchronise all four — we might push it back.
KRISTINA MY LINE
(stepping forward, stone raised) Then I go first. I'll draw its attention while you three get into position. It's what explorers do — we go where others won't.
AKBARHON
Kristina — please don't get eaten by a cosmic void. I'd really miss you.
KRISTINA MY LINE
(without looking back, already at the edge) Aww. I'd miss you too, Akbarhon. (the stone blazes in her hand) Now focus. The rift is moving. Get ready!
SCENE 04 Saving Tashkent

EXT. CHORSU ROOFTOP — DAWN. The void entity collapses under the combined starlight beam. The rift seals with a sound like distant thunder. Kristina watches it close, stone dimming in her hand, city lights flickering back to life below.

AMIRBEK
It's working. Don't let go — whatever you do, don't let go!
KRISTINA MY LINE
The rift is collapsing! Thirty percent… fifty… it's sealing!
SHAHINA
Signal confirmed — the entity is retreating. The stars… they're answering back. We did it.
KRISTINA MY LINE
(exhales slowly, watches the last of the rift dissolve into starlight) I've stood at the edge of a lot of cliffs. But nothing… nothing like that.
AKBARHON
So… same time next week?
KRISTINA MY LINE
(laughs — a real, relieved laugh) Ask me again after I sleep for three days. (looks out at the city) Tashkent. Still here. Still ours.
