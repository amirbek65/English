SCENE 01 Discovering the Stones

EXT. OLD TASHKENT ALLEYWAY — DUSK. Akbarhon trails behind the group, backpack clinking with half-built gadgets. He nearly trips on the crumbled pavement — and that's when everyone sees the glow rising from the rubble.

AMIRBEK
Wait — everyone stop. Do you hear that? It's coming from the ground.
SHAHINA
A voice? Amirbek, you're hearing things. It's probably a broken gas pipe or some underground cable.
KRISTINA
No, wait — she's wrong. I hear it too. Something is whispering. And those stones… they're glowing.
AMIRBEK
We pick them up. All four of us, at the same time. On three. One… two… three!
AKBARHON MY LINE
Bold plan, fearless leader! What could possibly go wrong? Other than, you know, an alien takeover?
AMIRBEK
Akbarhon — serious, please! It's warm. Like it's been waiting for us.
AKBARHON MY LINE
(holding up the stone, which hums against his palm) Okay, seriously though — my scanner is going berserk. This thing is emitting a frequency I've never seen. Also it tickles. Is it supposed to tickle?
SHAHINA
Thermal reading is off the charts. These aren't minerals from Earth.
AKBARHON MY LINE
Great! Unknown alien rocks. I'll just add that to the list of things that are definitely not dangerous. Right after the time I built a rocket out of a vacuum cleaner.
SCENE 02 Decoding the Star Messages

INT. SHAHINA'S BEDROOM — NIGHT. Akbarhon has assembled a cobbled-together device that somehow interfaces with the stones. He claims it was "all planned." Shahina looks at it and visibly winces. It works anyway.

SHAHINA
I've isolated the frequency — whoever sent this is not from Earth.
AMIRBEK
Target for what? Are they warning us, or marking us?
AKBARHON MY LINE
My stone just got hotter. And it showed me something — a dark mass, spiraling toward the solar system. That's not a metaphor, by the way.
SHAHINA
The window opens in… fourteen hours.
AKBARHON MY LINE
Fourteen hours. Cool, cool. Plenty of time for panic. (claps hands together) Right. I've already started building something. I call it the Resonance Amplifier. Shahina, don't look at it directly.
SHAHINA
(looks at it, immediately looks away) Akbarhon, is that duct tape holding together a satellite dish and a blender?
AKBARHON MY LINE
A very important blender. (pause) It also makes smoothies. Multi-purpose. Very efficient. (the device hums perfectly) See? Genius.
SCENE 03 Cosmic Danger

EXT. CHORSU ROOFTOP — MIDNIGHT. The void entity descends. The city below has gone eerily quiet. Akbarhon's gadget whirs frantically. For once, the jokes come out smaller — because this is, clearly, the real thing.

KRISTINA
It's here. It's actually here. The rift is 200 metres wide and growing. Amirbek — what do we do?!
AMIRBEK
We stand our ground. If we run, everyone in this city disappears!
SHAHINA
The stones resonate at the same frequency as the entity. If we synchronise all four — we might push it back.
AKBARHON MY LINE
For the record, I always knew my life would end on a rooftop. I just assumed it involved a kite.
AMIRBEK
Focus! Give it everything you've got. Every last bit.
AKBARHON MY LINE
What if it doesn't work? What if this is just how it ends? (quiet moment) I don't want it to end. Not yet. There are too many things I haven't built yet.
SHAHINA
It will work. The mathematics are sound. I trust the data. Now I need to trust us.
AKBARHON MY LINE
(nods, clutches stone tightly, and actually looks serious for the first time) Okay. Let's save Tashkent. Again, for the record — this was all part of my plan.
SCENE 04 Saving Tashkent

EXT. CHORSU ROOFTOP — DAWN. The void dissolves. The rift seals. Silence floods back. Akbarhon's device sparks once, then dies. He doesn't care. He's staring at the sunrise over Tashkent, and for maybe the first time, he has nothing to say.

AMIRBEK
It's working. Don't let go — whatever you do, don't let go!
KRISTINA
The rift is collapsing! Thirty percent… fifty… it's sealing!
SHAHINA
Signal confirmed — the entity is retreating. The stars… they're answering back. We did it.
AKBARHON MY LINE
So… same time next week? (everyone laughs, exhausted and relieved)
AMIRBEK
Tashkent is still standing. And so are we. That's enough. That's everything.
AKBARHON MY LINE
(looks at the now-dead device in his hands, then tosses it gently into his bag) You know what's funny? The blender part actually worked perfectly. Not a single smoothie was harmed in saving the galaxy.
AKBARHON MY LINE
(pause — looks at the glowing stone, now dim, nestled in his palm) Thanks for picking me, little rock. I didn't think I'd be the type. But… here we are.
